Our Vision

To establish an excellent team of scientists that would contribute to the development of products and toolkits to reduce the burden of diseases in our society.

Our mission

To close the capacity gap in research at individual, institutional and regional levels by developing a critical mass of competent researchers, providing an enabling environment for high quality research, and forging partnerships with industry.

Objectives

↠ Establish a pipeline of highly trained scientists at the MSc and PhD levels in clinical trials, genomics, molecular biology, pharmacological biochemistry and other medical research, who will serve as a bridge between Academia and Industry
↠ Create a platform for continuous product development
↠ Develop a strong infectious disease database to inform and modify policy with strong and verifiable evidence
↠ Institute several lines of health systems research
↠ Act as a honest broker between University, Industry and the Community in an equitable way

He also holds a Doctor of Medical Science Degree (Dr.Med.Sc.) of the Karolinska Institutet, Sweden and was visiting Research Fellow and Lecturer in the Liverpool School of Medicine in 1987. His work in malaria gave him the opportunity to be part of the global team that developed new antimalarials which included Halofantrine and Artemisinin, putting him on the world map with respect to management of severe and complicated malaria.
He joined the WHO in 1996 as Advisor on Operational Research for the Accelerated Malaria Control Programme. He served in various capacities such as WHO Representative to Uganda, WHO Coordinator for East and Southern Africa as well as West Africa. He was awarded the Order of the Knights of Burkina Faso in 2014 for his work in Public Health. He retired from WHO in 2014, and is currently a reviewer for WHO-TDR. He is also the head of the Babcock University Centre for Advanced Medical Research and Biotechnology (CAMRAB).
